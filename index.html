Whole Brain Paragraph

What motivates me more than anything across all of my interests is the importance of autonomy and personal freedom. This motivation informs my interest in programming. As long as we have access to a free and open internet, we can realistically learn from and communicate with anyone, which I believe fundamentally supports personal freedom and autonomy. My fascination with bikes ties into this motivation as well: I think the self-sufficiency and independence that bikes allow can be enabling for people. My partner spent soe time teaching English to children in Tanzania, and she has described to me how some of the kids would run three to five miles to school in the morning so that they could make it to class on time. The power of a bicycle to enable a person in that situation is huge. I view the autonomy of transportation provided by bicycles as an analog to the autonomy of expression and communication facilitated by the free and open internet.

Weekly Reflections

Week 1

I had a great time this first week of class. I'm really enjoying the pace and intensity that's been established so far, and I'm looking forward to things getting more difficult. A lot of the material on the DOM and front-end code I was already pretty familiar with, but the UI/UX and data design stuff is new to me and is more complex than I anticipated. I'm having a bit of a hard time fully grasping how to think of data relations in the way they're asking us to, but I'm definitely finding it interesting to think about data in this way. Just an an aside, I can tell that I'm genuinely enjoying myself because I had several moments on Saturday where I caught myself wishing I were still in class!

Also feeling like I made the right call deciding to take the train down from Santa Fe rather than driving. It's been very helpful to still be able to keep up with my mindfulness/reading practice in the mornings, and it's great having a couple hours on the way home to work on things for class.

Week 2

Week 2 was another busy week, we made significant progress with our Object Oriented projects and brainstormed capstone project ideas. Looking forward to seeing who is on my team, and what capstone project we will be working on.

I'm a little ahead of the class right now, I was able to get most of the Object Oriented stuff done fairly quickly. I'm already finished with Phase 2, which is due this upcoming Wednesday morning. Paul asked me to write up some more example classes for the ERDs we made in the data design project to give me some more practice with this sort of thing. Hoping to get a good shot at those done over the weekend.

An apprenticeship with Twitter came across my radar this week, and I'm going to apply for it. It would be an engineer internship either in San Francisco or in Boulder, with the opportunity to continue on at Twitter if the internship goes well. I'm really hoping I can get noticed for this, as it would be a huge step forward for me. Deadline is July 31, so I have a bit of time, but I'll need to manage my time well to fit this into everything else that we are doing.

Week 3

This week covered a lot of ground.

We got assigned PWP Milestone 2a, due this upcoming Thursday. I've gotten a good start on this milestone already -- I'm pretty sure my wireframes are good to go. I just need to think about the plan for my content sections.

We covered git branching and git-flow. This was really cool to learn, as I've always wondered if there was a proper system for dealing with branching. It makes sense, looking back at it now, that a project would want to have an entirely separate master branch for production-level code, with all of the development being done in discrete stages above the master branch. I'm looking forward to getting more comfortable with this type of work-flow.

We began talking about unit testing this week as well. I anticipate this being a pretty difficult thing to pick up. It seems like a very abstract way of thinking about our functions. I'm excited to get started with some unit tests of my own soon.

We also had a very brief introduction to CSS. I was rather surprised with how cursory of a look we got at the entire system. I think part of the issue was a time constraint, but CSS is a big part of front-end development. I'd have thought we'd get into greater detail. I guess they must just be confident that we will be able to figure it out as we go. That was definitely my experience working on the CSS challenge over the weekend. In retrospect I think that I should have utilized flexbox for this project, but I am still rather uncomfortable with it so I just messed around with margins/padding/floats. Definitely want to improve my flexbox/grid skills in the future.

For our capstone we were able to burn down three tickets on Friday, which is great. We're in a good position going into the second week of sprint 1 -- I'm optimistic we can get a head start on sprint 2 later this week.

Also, I'm submitting the Twitter apprenticeship application this week. I'm pretty happy with my cover letter and the current iteration of my resume. I'm pretty confident I'll at least get a phone screen.

Week 4

This week we had a lot of open work time, which was great. We have a lot of work to do on our capstone project. Monday we went over the CSS challenge, which was pretty informative. Whoever wrote the solution used much fewer lines of code than I did, but I was pretty close in the end. I could definitely use some more practice with CSS. I think my primary focus needs to be developing a system-level plan for laying out my pages. I was pretty much winging it for the CSS challenge (and for most CSS that I've written), and I can tell that having a pre-determined system for how to lay out a page makes everything much easier.

Bootstrap challenge is this weekend, I'm fairly confident that I'll be able to get through this one without issue. Bootstrap really does make grid-based layouts, like the mockups provided for the challenge, much simpler. I hope to get a pretty close copy done for this challenge.

Thursday and Friday I ran into pretty significant merge conflicts when trying to merge some of my branches back into my team's develop branch. I'm not sure where exactly the issues are coming from, but I think we all could do better at learning how to properly compartmentalize our work so that merge conflicts like this don't come up in the future. All of that being said, I'm glad that these issues are coming up in week 4, hopefully by the time we get to week 10 this will all feel fairly academic.
